Why West Pokot?

West Pokot is a vibrant, resilient region in Kenya, yet faces significant challenges in digital access and education. RuralShiftAfrica is dedicated to bridging this gap, empowering local youth—especially girls—with the skills and confidence to thrive in the digital age. Our programs are tailored to the unique needs and dreams of West Pokot's communities.

Turning Challenges Into Opportunities

In West Pokot, children and youth often face disruptions to their education due to cattle raids and harmful cultural practices. These challenges can lead to lost opportunities and put young people at risk of negative influences. RuralShiftAfrica offers a safe, inspiring alternative—keeping children and youth engaged, building digital skills, and opening doors to brighter futures. By shifting focus from conflict to creativity, we help young people discover their potential and become positive change-makers in their communities.

Our Purpose

Mission

To bridge the digital divide in rural Kenya by providing safe, creative alternatives to conflict and negative traditions. We empower the children and youth of West Pokot with tech skills, keeping them engaged, inspired, and ready to build positive futures for themselves and their communities.

Vision

A generation of digitally empowered children and youth rising from West Pokot, creating solutions for local challenges and competing in the global digital economy—inspiring peace, progress, and hope.

Our Story

March 2025 – Humble Beginnings

Inspired by the growing digital divide in West Pokot, the idea of RuralShiftAfrica was born. A small team began dreaming of a community-based tech initiative focused on equipping disadvantaged children and girls with essential digital skills.

April 2025 – Call for Support

Outreach officially began for sponsors, donors, and volunteers to bring the vision to life. We are seeking laptops, funding, internet access, and teaching support to launch our very first community class.
"With just one laptop, we could spark a future. Imagine what ten can do."

Future Goals – Expanding Reach

Once launched, our goal is to establish community-based learning centers and mobile tech labs to serve even the most remote areas of West Pokot.

Future Vision – Girls Who Code Pokot

We're planning a dedicated program to encourage young girls to embrace coding, STEM, and leadership through hands-on learning, mentorship, and storytelling from female tech leaders.

Next Milestone – First Launch

With your support, we aim to launch our first training center by late 2025. Every donation, laptop, and helping hand brings us closer to that goal.

Our Founder

Irene Cherono grew up in West Pokot, where she saw firsthand the challenges facing children and youth. After high school, she moved to Nairobi to pursue a degree in computer science. Inspired by her experiences and education, Irene returned home with a vision: to empower her community through technology and education, helping children and youth build brighter, more hopeful futures.
